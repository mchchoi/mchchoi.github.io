I am an applied and computational probabilist broadly interested in and fascinated by Markov chains, Markov processes, stochastic algorithms and their applications in a diverse range of areas such as statistical physics, stochastic optimization, Bayesian statistics, information theory, game theory and theoretical computer science. I am currently an Assistant Professor at both Department of Statistics and Data Science at National University of Singapore (NUS) and Yale-NUS College. I am also affiliated with the Institute of Operations Research and Analytics (IORA). Prior to joining NUS in July 2021, I have worked at the School of Data Science (SDS) at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen. I graduated from the School of Operations Research and Information Engineering (ORIE) at Cornell University. My advisor is Prof. Pierre Patie. Prior to Cornell, I received my undergraduate degree in Actuarial Science at The University of Hong Kong. I currently serve as an Associate Editor (August 2023 - Present) of Statistics and Computing.

Research interests

  • Markov chains, Markov processes: theory and applications
  • Stochastic algorithms: Markov chain Monte Carlo, Metropolis-Hastings, simulated annealing, Langevin dynamics, Hamiltonian Monte Carlo, Sequential Monte Carlo
  • Markov chains + X, where X can be statistical physics, applied probability, stochastic optimization (particularly global, discrete, combinatorial, integer, submodular or continuous), mathematical and Bayesian statistics (particularly variational inference), information theory (particularly information projection, geometry, mutual information and rate-distortion theory), theoretical computer science (particularly sum-of-squares and semidefinite programming), game theory, linear algebra (particularly matrix perturbation theory, matrix nearness problem and Sylvester's equation), functional analysis (particularly spectral theory, best approximation theory), quantum computation (particularly quantum annealing), group theory, control theory (particularly risk-sensitive control of Markov chains), partial differential equations, dynamical systems, geometry (particularly discrete and computational), protein folding, molecular dynamics, computational chemistry, kinetic theory...
